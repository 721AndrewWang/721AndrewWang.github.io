1️⃣In the Sandwich Technique, the teacher starts with a target teaching sentence, which is like a slice of bread➡️[Take care (while I leave.)].

2️⃣Next comes a brief insertion of the meaning in the mother tongue, which acts like the meat and veggies in the sandwich➡️[안녕히 계세요.], providing quick comprehension support.

3️⃣Finally, the teacher closes with the same target teaching sentence, forming the other slice of bread➡️[Take care (while I leave).].

The Sandwich Technique in Psychology and Communication

The sandwich technique is also applied in communication and psychology, particularly in delivering feedback effectively. This method places constructive criticism between two positive statements, aiming to reduce defensiveness and increase receptivity.

Example in Feedback

"I appreciate your effort on this project. However, I noticed some inconsistencies in data analysis. I am confident you can refine this with minor adjustments."

Benefits

  • Encourages openness to criticism
  • Reduces defensiveness
  • Enhances motivation for improvement

Criticism of the Sandwich Technique

Despite its advantages, the Sandwich Technique has faced criticism for potentially undermining the effectiveness of feedback. Some argue that:

  • 🎭 It may cause recipients to perceive positive feedback as insincere.
  • 🗿 If the core criticism is not clearly articulated, it can lead to misunderstandings.
  • 🏂 Many individuals prefer direct and unambiguous feedback without unnecessary cushioning.

As an alternative, transparent communication strategies that foster open dialogue may be more effective than rigidly structuring feedback in a layered format.

In the context of Second Language Acquisition (SLA), embedding a familiar phrase between new phrases can help learners integrate new vocabulary more effectively while ensuring rapid comprehension.

However, this method should be used selectively⚠️. Over-reliance on translation can hinder contextual learning, which plays a key role in language development.

Most language input for young learners can be understood through contextual cues📰, reducing the need for direct translation. Nevertheless, when rapid meaning clarification is necessary, 💪 the Sandwich Technique serves as a useful tool for efficient communication while maintaining immersion in the target language.

For effective feedback delivery in education and professional settings, educators and managers can apply principles of constructive feedback, ensuring clarity while promoting growth.
